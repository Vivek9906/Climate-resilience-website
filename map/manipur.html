Manipur Pollution Information

Here you can find information about pollution levels in Manipur.

Cause of Pollution:

Industrial Pollution: While Manipur's industrial sector is relatively small, emissions from industries, such as brick kilns and small-scale manufacturing units, contribute to air pollution.
Vehicle Emissions: The growing number of vehicles, particularly in urban areas like Imphal, leads to emissions of carbon monoxide (CO), nitrogen oxides (NOx), and particulate matter, contributing to air pollution.
Biomass Burning: Traditional practices such as burning of agricultural waste and wood for cooking and heating purposes contribute to localized air pollution in rural areas.

The air pollution condition in Manipur is relatively moderate compared to other states, but efforts are being made to address sources of pollution and improve air quality.

Future Outlook

To improve air quality in Manipur, various measures are being considered:

Promote Clean Energy: Encourage the adoption of cleaner cooking technologies and renewable energy sources to reduce reliance on biomass burning.
Enhance Public Transport: Improve public transportation systems and promote the use of electric vehicles to reduce vehicular emissions.
Community Awareness: Raise awareness among communities about the health impacts of air pollution and promote sustainable practices.
Regulatory Measures: Enforce regulations to control emissions from industries and vehicles, and promote eco-friendly practices.
